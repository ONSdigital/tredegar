Welcome to the ONS prototype website

The primary objective of this prototype was to build a broad but shallow version of the ONS website that utilises modern web design thinking and allows us to implement the insights we have gained from our user research throughout the ‘discovery’ phase and beyond.

What content will I find in this prototype?

What do we mean by ‘broad but shallow’? We've tried to provide enough content in the prototype to allow some kind of endpoint for the majority of user journeys. This means that there is appropriate content in all of the navigation ‘nodes’ (we've implemented the taxonomy we previously tested) and a wide spread of content types.

What content won't I find?

You won't find the entirety of our datasets and time series, and only a selection of our statistical bulletins and other article types. Historic data (other than ‘time series’) and some sections (About us, Methodology, etc.) are also less populated.

Content on this website is not conclusive and may not be up-to-date or correct. Please refer to the current ONS website for the most recent statistics.

Search

We have introduced a new approach to search using the open source software, ElasticSearch. This builds upon work we were hoping to implement on the current site and benefits from guidance from experts at independent search consultancy Flax and the Government Digital Service. Again the ‘broad but shallow’ approach has been important here in ensuring there is enough content to provide realistic search results.

We know a good search experience is hugely important to you and your feedback on the search solution you'll find here is invaluable in helping us build a search that works for you.

Built by our users

We've remained focused on our user needs throughout this project and used a combination of formal, lab based and informal ‘guerilla’ user research to build this website you're using today.

This process is an iterative one and whilst using the prototype you will notice links to provide feedback on your experience. Your observations WILL help shape the next ONS website so please feel free to provide as much feedback as you can.

Our development approach

We've formed a co-located, multidisciplinary team that work in an agile manner and use current best practice in all approaches. The team have made use of ‘cloud’ infrastructure and has implemented continuous integration and automated testing tools. We've practiced ‘test driven development’ and are using open source frameworks rather than reinventing the wheel. We were lucky enough to have been given the freedom to take an almost ‘lean startup’ approach and hopefully we've made the most of the learning opportunities.

Lots more to come

Through user research we have identified a number of areas that require further investigation and cannot be addressed within the scope of the prototype. These include, but are not limited to:

  • what we do with local and geographical data
  • access to all the time series data and datasets
  • improved terminology and language used throughout
  • alternate methods of accessing data

What's next and how can I help?

The prototype website will close on the 5th January 2015. We will review all feedback and user research findings before determining the next steps.

We really need your help during this time in one of the following ways:

  1. We'd like comments on structure, presentation or usability of this prototype and they'll be used to help us develop it further.
  2. Share with colleagues or anyone else you may feel be interested!
